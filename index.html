Punchy One-Line Headline in Title Case or All Caps

Write a two-line synopsis of your story here for a dek in sentence case. Not all stories will need deks. Make sure that the hed, dek, and lede are not too repetitous and that each adds something new.

Published on: April 13, 2024

By Newie Newhouse

Start with a one-sentence compelling lede - perhaps the start of an ancedote that illustrates the phenomon you are writing about, or a summary lede that sums up your most interesting findings.

Keep your paragraphs short. One sentence ledes (sometimes more in a magazine, ancedotal lede format) followed by paragraghs that will generally be 1-3 sentences long. Note that if you use a summary news lede, you probably don't want a dek.

"Format quotes like this," said Greg Munno, a journalism professor at Syracuse University. "Note that comma goes inside of the quation marks. I am also attributing after the first sentence rather than at the beginning or end of the quote."
